Identifying Sensitive Skin

Sensitive skin types may show characteristics of dry, oily, and combination skin types but they can also be subject to redness and irritation. Those with sensitive skin often have skin conditions such as rosacea and eczema, or even severe allergies. Those with sensitive skin need to take extra cautions with their skin, as many products and ingredients can easily irritate you.

Dermatologist Leslie Baumman says that people with sensitive skin should avoid friction, excessive heat and triggers like alcohol or stress. Other experts also see overlapping with sensitive and dry skin types, as both are prone to redness and irritation.

Sensitive skin can also be a result of overexfoliating, overscrubbing, and using harsh products.

“Most skin, if you use the right products, is not as sensitive as you think it is,” Dr. Doris Day, founder of Day Dermatology and Aesthetics, said.

Here are 10 signs that you could potentially have sensitive skin:

  1. Your skin easliy flushes
  2. You're prone to rashes and bumps
  3. Beauty products sting or burn
  4. You have dry patches
  5. Your skin feels itchy
  6. You're sensitive to UV rays
  7. You have broken capillaries
  8. Fragrance irritates your skin
  9. Your skin reacts to bad weather
  10. You break out easily
