Identifying Combination Skin

Those with combination skin types have characteristics of two or more types of skin. These skin types often have to use different types of products for various parts of the face and body. Combination skin is commonly described as being oily around the T-Zone (forehead, chin, and nose) and dry around the cheeks. Combination skin is also described as changing based on the seasons; for example, your skin could be oily during the summer and dry during the winter. However, there is no true definition for this skin type and it can mean different things for everyone.

This skintype can be confusing at times, as the way that the skin behaves is often inconsistent. In general, skintype is determined by many factors, such as genetics, hormone levels, and environmental factors.

Some signs that you might have combination skin include:

  1. Your t-zone is oily while other areas are dry – This is the most common identifier for combination skin. The areas might vary from each individual.
  2. Your t-zone has larger pores – Those with combination skin have a more oil t-zone compared to dry or normal skin. This means that your pores are bigger and more visible.
  3. You have dandruff – While not inherently tied with combination skin, many with this skin type also see a similar pattern on their scalp. Dandruff then crops up as some areas are naturally drier and flakier than others.
  4. Your cheeks feel tight after washing – This is a big indicator that you have combination skin, as it shows the differeing amounts of oil your skin produces. If this happens after washing your face, you probably noticed that your cheeks get flaky without moisturizer while other areas don’t seem to need extra help.
  5. Your acne is mostly concentrated around the T-zone – Extra oil and blocked pores means acne. If you experience the vast majority of your acne on your forehead or chin, or the majority of your blackheads around your nose, you probably have combination skin.
