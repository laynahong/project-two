Taking Care of Combination and Sensitive Skin Types

Based on your skin type, there are different approaches that you should take if you want to improve the condition of your skin. Here, you will learn about how to take care of sensitive and combination skin types!

Here are some tips on how to take care of your sensitive skin:

  • Take short 5 to 10 minute showers with warm — not hot — water
  • Avoid harsh astringents and exfoliants
  • Use a gentle, fragrance-free soap
  • Use essential oils instead of perfumes
  • Use a gentle, fragrance-free laundry detergent
  • Try using organic cleaning supplies
  • Always use a shaving cream or gel
  • Gently pat yourself dry after a shower (instead of rubbing) and apply moisturizer right away
  • Test new products on a discreet area of skin at least one day before trying a full-application

Here are some tips on how to take care of your combination skin:

  • Be sure to ditch any harsh cleansers you own – These cleansers completely strip everything from your skin and will completely destroy the drier patches on your cheeks and elsewhere. Stick to a gentle cleanser and use it morning and night to keep your oil under control without being overly-stripping.
  • Exfoliate to get rid of the dead skin around your T-zone – Just ensure you don’t do this too often! Once a week or so is plenty.
  • Blot away any excess oil that pops up throughout the day – This will keep your face looking perfect all day while also cutting down on the likelihood of breakouts, blackheads, whiteheads, and more.
  • Beyond anything, use a good moisturizer! – You might think that your oil zones might mean you are exempt from this step, but you definitely are not. A good, light moisturizer will go a long way to keeping your drier skin hydrated without overhydrating your oiler areas.

Skin type is determined by various genetic and external factors. For example, genetics determines how much oil your face produces, as well as any skin conditions you have. However, factors like the weather and what you use on your face also influence your skin condition.

Depending on external factors, your skin type can change! For example, some people will have oily skin during the summer and dry skin during the winter. Most people will not fit a single skin type description exactly. You might also have combination skin type.

Many experts recommend you do a patch test (testing a product on your hand first) to see if you have any inflammatory response before using a product.
